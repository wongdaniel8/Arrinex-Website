What is Chronic Rhinitis?
The Arrinex Solution

It's Common...

About 60-80 million Americans have either allergic or non-allergic rhinitis. The difference? One is triggered by allergies, the other is triggered by unknown causes

It's Annoying...

Both allergic and non-allergic rhinitis result in allergy symptoms such as a runny nose, itchy eyes and skin, sneezing, stuffy nose, and fatigue.

...It's Easily Triggered

Common triggers are pollen, grass, pet hair, and dust mites. Non-allergenic triggers include cleaning solutions, cigarette smoke, hair spray, car exhaust

  • Nasal rinsing

    is a process that involves squirting a saline solution up the nostrils to purge the nasal cavities. Nasal rinses can be slightly repulsive and unfomfortable. At best, nasal rinses only bring about temporary relief from certain symptoms of allergies.

  • Medication

    is often used to mask the symptoms of rhinitis by temporarily surpressing the release of histamines within the body. Even if medication is susccessful in reducing the irritating affects of rhinitis, they must be constantly replenished which can take a toll financially.

  • Steriod nasal sprays

    work by inhaling a liquid-aresol medicine up your nose for the purpose of reducing inflammation of the nasal lining. Depending on the type, some nasal sprays require several days to a week of continued use before any improvement in symptoms can be noticed.

  • Sinus surgery

    is used in for certain cases of chronic rhinits but is often seen as a last resort. Surgery can help drain fluid built up in the sinuses or remove nasal polyps, either of which could be the result of either form of rhinis.

  • Dealing with it.

    When the prospect of continually seeking medication or constantly trying different therapies seems to have no avail, many people are left with the unfortunate choice of continuing the laborious process of seeking relief, or just living with chronic rhinitis.
